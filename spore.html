Spore

Spore is a real-time simulation game where the player creates their own organism and helps them evolve through various means by accumulating 'dna points'. Although the game is wildly inaccurate when it comes to its depiction of evolution and how creatures are developed over time, accuracy isn't the main draw and is not the lens the game should be judged by when it comes to its value as a learning tool. The game should, rather, be judged by how it gathers intrigue from the player into the development of organisms and life science. Through environmental hardships and different adaptions, the player's creatures will evolve and this basic principle of needing to evolve in order to succeed and thrive is a good starting point for a player to start asking questions about evolution in our world.

DNA, the fundamental carrier of genetic information in most living creatures.

For the uninitiated, evolution is basically how living beings adapt over time to their environment. The traits of organisms that are able to reproduce are carried over to the next generation of offspring, and although in game the player is in control of this, if one views the player as the judge of what traits are deemed 'desirable', there is a parallel to be made between the process of evolution in real life versus that which is displayed in the game.

A depiction of the progress of evolution from early apes to our current species.

The player can really go wild with their creations, making big, small, beautiful, or ugly organisms that start life as tiny, near single-cell organisms that eventually become multi-cell organisms with multiple organells and new traits. This appeal to creativity and fun gameplay loop where the player can live through their organism.
