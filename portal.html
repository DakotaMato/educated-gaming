Portal

Portal is a first-person puzzle game created by Valve that asks the player to think with, well, portals. Although portals don't exist in real life, the nature of the puzzles demands logical thinking regarding non-euclidean space and a manipulation of physics in order to succeed in the game. Although it might be easy for some people, basic logical reasoning can be a struggle for many people, especially younger children. In this aspect, portal is a fun-filled way to help stimulate one's brain with fun puzzles.

A portal connecting two different areas together.

To give an example of a way in which Portal helps teach basic logic, one of the opening obstacles involves simple vertical traversal. The player is shown the portal gun (the device the player uses to create portals) shooting a portal in order to get them to this example puzzle, so the player knows how the gun works, but has yet to test it out. However, the game helps you since this is the first puzzle with the gun, as one of your two portals has already been placed for you on a ledge you cannot reach by simply jumping. All the player has to do to make it to the above area is place a portal in a location they can reach (which is most of the ground, even that beneath the player's feet) and walk through. This helps communicate to the player how the portals work and how to think outside the box.
