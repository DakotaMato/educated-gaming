Kerbal Space Program

Kerbal Space Program is a space-flight simulation game that uses a realistic physics engine to fuel its basic gameplay mechanics. The game is notable in that it is officially endorsed by NASA and the European Space Agency, so if that doesn't count for anything right off the bat, I'm not sure what will. Regardless, the basic goal of the game is to successfully launch rockets into outer space and go on further space missions to other planets and solar systems.

A rocket ship, typically used to fly into space.

Despite being the games' primary focus, you don't need to be a rocket scientist in order to succeed, although having an understanding of basic physics and engineering principles will help players on their journey throughout the game. The game teaches players about fundamental physics mechanics that are important considerations in aerospace missions such as fuel-to-weight ratios, orbital modelling, and gravity through the in-game interface telling you in real-time how said mechanics are affecting your space-craft. Unlike real life, though, the player isn't losing millions of dollars when things go wrong, which invites them to experiment and really think about what might have gone wrong with their rocket design and reconsider if they truly understand all the underlying physics mechanics in play. The wacky visuals also make for an inviting atmosphere that can help ease frustration if you go multiple attempts without a success.
